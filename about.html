EB Games

From Sales Associate to Store Manager

Sales Associate/Keyholder - Sept 2016 to Aug 2019

I was hired at EB Games in 2016 as a sales associate providing excellent customer service by selling video games, accessories, warranties, loyalty cards and loot. I handled financial transactions including cash, debit and credit as well as trade ins. Approximately 3 months into the job I was given the position of Keyholder. I was responsible for opening and closing the store, ordering supplies, accurately processing shipping and receiving, and maintaining merchandise. During this time I consistently remained a top performer in sales by building rapport with customers. Approximately 1 year into the job, I took on the role of acting Assistant Manager. I maintained this roll for 2 years, helping out in multiple locations, before getting promoted to Assistant Manager.

Skills

  • Customer Service

  • Time Management

Assistant Manager - Aug 2019 to July 2020

As an Assistant Manager, I was responsible for many things including hiring and training employees, carrying out coaching plans, and executing write ups. I became skilled at resolving customer issues by going above and beyond to ensure customer satisfaction while maintaining company policy. I collaborated with Sony and Nintendo representatives for marketing and special events such as Pokemon card release days or midnight lauches for games. I planned, organized, directed and evaluated not only daily operations, but every event as well. Customers claimed that my midnight lauches were some of the most fun launches they experienced in years! When I wasn't on the floor talking to customers, I was attending conference calls, handling and logging paperwork, preparing weekly marketing, and executing visual merchandising.

Store Manager - July 2020 to April 2021

I became a store manager during the pandemic and was given a store that was not performing well. I retrained the staff, hired new staff, re-merchandised the entire store, organized paperwork, and brought the store metrics up to company standard. During this time, I also helped out to run another store who didn't have a manager while also helping to train the Assistant Manager. As a manager, I learned a lot about the importance of having good relationships with staff to ensure a good working environment, how to work with different personality types, and how to delegate tasks that maximized efficiency within each persons skill set.
